# Phase 6: Act

## Recommendation 1: Market the Time watch around consistency

In this data, activity is uneven across the week, dropping off on Friday and Sunday in particular. This suggests users would benefit more from a nudge to stay steady than from a push toward a certain step count which most days isn't being clearing anyway. Bellabeat could position the Time watch's coaching features around weekly consistency messaging rather than a daily step target.

## Recommendation 2: Use sedentary time as the primary sleep-health signal

Sedentary minutes correlated with poor sleep far more strongly than total steps did, which means the Time watch's value proposition for sleep should be "sit less." A marketing angle built around breaking up long sedentary stretches using standing reminders or short movement breaks flagged through the day is more directly tied to the strongest pattern in the data. This also differentiates the Time watch from competitors that lean entirely on step-count marketing, since it lets Bellabeat make a specific, evidence-backed claim about sleep quality.

## Recommendation 3: Time engagement around the 6 PM activity window

Since users are already most active in the early evening, that's the highest-leverage moment for the Time watch to reinforce behavior. Marketing and in-app content should be timed around this window instead of spread evenly through the day, as a message at 6 PM lands when someone is already moving. This also opens a natural product narrative of the Time watch as something that meets users at the moment they're already active, not one more app trying to interrupt their day.
